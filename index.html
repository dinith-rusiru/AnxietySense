Our Domain

Understanding the landscape of digital mental health and the gap ANXIETYSENSE fills

Research Background

Anxiety disorders affect approximately 301 million people worldwide (WHO). Existing digital mental health tools adopt undifferentiated, one-size-fits-all approaches that fail to reflect the life-stage-specific nature of anxiety — ignoring the unique physiological, psychological, and social characteristics of each demographic group.

Research Problem

Most deployed mental health applications rely on a single modality and serve an undifferentiated adult population, leaving children, pregnant women, and elderly individuals without tools addressing their specific needs, communication styles, or digital access constraints. A seven-year-old's anxiety differs markedly from a pregnant woman's perinatal anxiety or an elderly person's health-related worry.

Methodology

A structured, interdisciplinary methodology integrating quantitative model development, expert clinical validation, and iterative mobile application design. Developed in collaboration with licensed clinical psychologists who contributed to dataset annotation, assessment instrument validation, and evaluation of application outputs — with ethical approval from the institutional review board.



Literature Survey

Deep convolutional neural networks trained on large-scale facial expression datasets such as FER-2013 and AffectNet have demonstrated strong generalisation to real-world emotion recognition tasks, with state-of-the-art models achieving over 70% accuracy on the seven-class expression benchmark. Transfer learning from lightweight architectures such as MobileNet has enabled deployment of capable emotion classifiers on mobile devices with acceptable inference latency, making them suitable for embedded mental health applications.

The use of children's drawings as diagnostic instruments has a long history in clinical psychology, with standardised systems such as the Draw-a-Person test providing frameworks for inferring emotional state from graphic features including figure size, spatial placement, colour use, and figure completeness. Computational approaches to drawing analysis have begun to emerge with promising accuracy results.

Voice-based emotion recognition using LSTM networks has proven effective at modelling the temporal dynamics of speech, capturing prosodic variation and energy fluctuations associated with emotional arousal and valence. Datasets such as RAVDESS and IEMOCAP provide benchmarks for speech emotion recognition. The GAD-7 has been extensively validated as a brief screening instrument for generalised anxiety disorder across diverse populations including elderly cohorts. Random Forest classifiers have shown particular robustness on small clinical tabular datasets due to their ensemble nature and resistance to overfitting.

Research Gap

Despite advances in AI-powered mental health tools, our analysis reveals several critical gaps. First, the majority of deployed applications rely on a single modality (e.g., facial expression alone or questionnaire alone), losing the complementary information encoded across multiple data streams.

Second, there is a persistent lack of demographic specificity. Children's limited verbal self-report capability, the physical constraints of pregnant women, and the lower digital literacy of elderly users are rarely addressed in a single unified platform. Existing tools are predominantly designed for working-age adults.

Third, the broader literature highlights persistent gaps in clinical validation — most mobile mental health applications have not been evaluated in partnership with licensed clinical professionals. ANXIETYSENSE directly addresses these gaps through its modular, demographically-segmented architecture, multi-modal fusion strategies, and rigorous expert clinical validation process involving three psychologists and two counselors.

Research Objectives

ANXIETYSENSE addresses the identified demographic and modality gaps through the following objectives:

  • Develop a child anxiety detection module combining real-time facial expression recognition (MobileNet V2) with a drawing-pattern ML classifier, producing a dual-modality anxiety index validated by licensed child psychologists.
  • Build a young adult anxiety assessment pipeline integrating CNN-based facial emotion detection with a GAD-7 adapted conversational chatbot, using sentiment analysis and confidence-weighted modality fusion.
  • Design a voice-based anxiety screening module for pregnant women using LSTM networks trained on MFCC, pitch, and energy features extracted from voice recordings, prioritising high-anxiety recall for screening utility.
  • Create a simplified, accessible GAD-7 assessment interface for elderly users with voice-reading support, backed by a Random Forest classifier trained on 312 counselor-validated assessments.
  • Integrate all four modules into a unified React Native mobile platform with a Firebase backend, a personalised recommendation engine delivering therapeutic activities, and longitudinal anxiety tracking for users and caregivers.

Key Features

Specialised AI modules delivering life-stage-appropriate anxiety detection and support

Child Anxiety Module

Dual-modality approach combining MobileNet V2 facial expression recognition across five affective categories with a drawing pattern CNN classifier. Late fusion of both streams yields 85% combined accuracy, outperforming each sub-model individually.

Young Adult Module

A five-layer CNN for real-time facial emotion classification fused with a GAD-7 adapted conversational chatbot applying NLP sentiment analysis. Confidence-weighted multimodal fusion achieves 88% composite accuracy.

Pregnant Women Module

Voice-based anxiety screening using a two-layer LSTM network (128 & 64 units) trained on 40-dimensional MFCC vectors, pitch contours, and energy envelopes. Achieves 82% recall on the clinically-prioritised high-anxiety class.

Elderly Module

GAD-7 questionnaire administered via a large-text, voice-reading-supported interface. A Random Forest classifier (200 estimators) trained on 312 clinical assessments achieves 98% accuracy with interpretable feature importance output.

Personalised Recommendations

A unified recommendation engine delivers tailored therapeutic activities per demographic group and severity level — including guided breathing, mindfulness, journaling, CBT prompts, prenatal relaxation, and caregiver alerts for high-severity cases.

Firebase & Longitudinal Tracking

Encrypted real-time Firebase backend enables secure data storage, cross-session progress tracking, and longitudinal anxiety trend monitoring — allowing users, caregivers, and clinicians to monitor severity changes over time.

Technologies Used

Cutting-edge AI, mobile, and cloud technologies powering ANXIETYSENSE

React Native

Cross-platform mobile development delivering a seamless Android experience with a single unified codebase across all four demographic modules.

Firebase

Encrypted real-time data storage, authentication, and longitudinal progress tracking backend supporting the unified recommendation engine.

TensorFlow & TF Lite

Deep learning model development in TensorFlow with Google Colab training, exported to TensorFlow Lite for efficient on-device inference.

scikit-learn

Random Forest classifier (200 estimators, stratified k-fold cross-validation) for the elderly GAD-7 module with interpretable feature importance analysis.

LSTM · Voice Analysis

Two-layer LSTM network processing 40-dimensional MFCC vectors, fundamental frequency contours, and zero-crossing rates across sliding 25ms windows.

MobileNet V2 · CNN

Parameter-efficient MobileNet V2 for mobile facial emotion recognition, plus custom CNN architecture for drawing pattern classification in the child module.
